01 — Foundation

What is GST?

Core Definition
Goods & Service Tax
A unified indirect tax levied by the Government on every type of good or service. It replaced VAT (Value Added Tax) and was launched in India on 1st July 2017. GST is a single tax that covers the entire supply chain — from manufacturer to end consumer.
ℹ️
Before GST — VAT Era Prior to July 2017, India used a Value Added Tax (VAT) system. Each state had different rates and rules making inter-state trade complex. GST replaced this with a unified structure.

🛒 Goods

Any tangible product that can be bought or sold — electronics, food, clothing, medicines, etc. Every physical item that changes hands is classified as a "Good."

🔧 Services

Any non-physical benefit or work provided — mobile recharge, internet, hotel stay, repair work, etc. Anything you pay for that isn't a physical product is a "Service."

🎯
Exam Key Point GST is a destination-based tax — it is collected at the point of final consumption, not at the point of production.
02 — Business Classification

Types of Business

Under GST, every business falls into one of two fundamental categories. Understanding which type you belong to determines your registration requirements, invoicing rules, and tax treatment.

📦

Purchase & Sales of Goods

Businesses that buy and sell physical products for profit. The entire cycle involves physical goods changing hands.

Electronics Shop Mobile Store Grocery Shop Clothing Store Pharmacy
⚙️

Service Providing Business

Businesses where no physical goods are exchanged. Revenue is earned by providing a service or skill.

Mobile Recharge Internet Service Hotel / Restaurant Item Repairing Consulting
✅
Quick Identification Rule Ask yourself: "Am I delivering a physical item or providing a skill/benefit?" If physical — it's Goods. If non-physical — it's a Service.
03 — Registration

Who Must Register for GST?

Business Type Annual Turnover Threshold GST Registration
Goods (Products) ₹40 Lakh+ 🔴 Mandatory
Services ₹20 Lakh+ 🔴 Mandatory
Below threshold Any amount 🟢 Optional (Voluntary)
⚠️
Important Note Even if your turnover is below the threshold, you can voluntarily register for GST. Once registered, you must maintain complete GST records and may need to hire an accountant.

📋 Documents Required for GST Registration

Apply at gst.gov.in with these documents. After approval (3–5 days), you receive your GSTIN number.

  • Aadhar Card of Applicant
  • PAN Card of Applicant
  • Passport Size Photo
  • Bank Account Front Page (Cancelled Cheque)
  • Valid Mobile Number
  • Proof of Business Address (Electricity Bill etc.)
  • Rent Agreement (if shop is on rent)
  • Firm Name / Business Name

06 — Mechanism

How GST Works

Input Tax
Tax paid while purchasing
When a GST-registered firm buys goods, the tax included in the purchase bill is called Input Tax. This amount is deposited with the government — but you can recover it from your customers when you sell.
Output Tax
Tax collected while selling
When the registered firm sells goods, the tax charged on the sale bill is Output Tax. The difference between Output Tax and Input Tax — i.e., the tax on your profit — is paid to the government.

07 — Invoice Types

SGST · CGST · IGST

SGST State Goods & Service Tax
Collected by the State Government. Applied on intra-state transactions.
50% of Total GST Rate
CGST Central Goods & Service Tax
Collected by the Central Government. Applied on intra-state transactions.
50% of Total GST Rate
IGST Integrated Goods & Service Tax
Collected by Central Govt on inter-state transactions.
100% of Total GST Rate
📌
When to use which? Same State → SGST + CGST (split 50/50). Different States → IGST (full rate). For example: buying from UP and selling in UP = SGST + CGST. Buying from UP and selling in Punjab = IGST only.

08 — Summary

Key Takeaways

01
GST = Goods & Service TaxLaunched 1 July 2017. Replaced VAT. Destination-based unified indirect tax applicable across India.
02
5 Rate Slabs0%, 5%, 12%, 18%, 28%. Most daily-use goods are at 0–5%; electronics and software at 12–18%; luxury items at 28%.
03
Registration ThresholdGoods: ₹40 Lakh/year. Services: ₹20 Lakh/year. Below threshold is voluntary. GSTIN = 15-digit unique number.
04
SGST + CGST vs IGSTIntra-state sales split tax 50/50 between state and centre. Inter-state sales pay full IGST to the Centre, which distributes it.
05
Input Tax Credit (ITC)You claim back tax paid on purchases against tax collected on sales. Only the profit's tax goes to government.
06
In Tally — Purchase Return = Debit NoteWhen goods are returned to supplier. Sales Return = Credit Note. Both adjust the original GST liability.
